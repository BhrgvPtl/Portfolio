Urban Data Warehouse for Spatial Analytics

Designed a lakehouse that blends GIS layers with temporal sensor data, powering planners with faster geospatial analyses and scenario modeling.

Role

Data Engineer

Timeline

10 weeks

Impact

40% faster spatial queries • 30+ curated civic datasets

Business Problem

City planners received siloed data drops from transportation, zoning, and utilities teams. Each dataset had unique coordinate systems, refresh cadences, and governance rules. Analysts spent hours wrangling spreadsheets before they could answer questions about mobility programs, capital projects, or resilience planning.

Approach

  1. Established data governance and metadata standards across contributing agencies.
  2. Ingested shapefiles, sensor feeds, and tabular data into a Delta Lake-backed bronze layer.
  3. Leveraged Spark to normalize coordinate reference systems and harmonize temporal granularity.
  4. Modeled star- and snowflake-based marts optimized for regional and temporal slicing.
  5. Built incremental materializations with dbt to serve analysts through BI dashboards.

Tools & Methods

  • Apache Spark
  • Delta Lake
  • dbt
  • PostGIS
  • QGIS
  • Tableau

Data Story

With harmonized data, planners could analyze pedestrian volumes against crash reports, overlay zoning petitions with flood risks, and evaluate how pilot projects shifted commute times. Storytelling dashboards translated complex GIS computations into intuitive metrics for public meetings and executive briefings.

Results

  • Reduced geospatial query time by 40% using partitioning by region and time windows.
  • Cut manual data preparation from 6 hours to 45 minutes with reusable transformation macros.
  • Enabled near real-time KPI tracking for bike lane utilization and congestion mitigation.
  • Delivered documentation and training that onboarded 15 analysts in under two weeks.

Productionization

Spark jobs run on autoscaling clusters orchestrated through Airflow, while dbt manages testing (freshness, relationships, accepted values) and documentation. Access policies enforce least privilege across departments, and Terraform codifies storage, compute, and IAM resources for reproducibility.
